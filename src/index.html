Support employees taking climate action on 21 May 2021

Over 3,000 Australian businesses participated in Not Business As Usual as part of the 2019 climate strikes.

On 21 May this year, students are organising the first climate strike since then, in opposition to the proposed gas-fired economic recovery.

Businesses are invited to close doors again, to allow employees to support the students and strike. This moment is an opportunity for businesses to put climate back on the agenda and say that it’s Not Business As Usual to fix the economy by accelerating the climate crisis.

Pledge Now

Why the strike matters

This strike is held 10 days following the federal budget, where it is expected that there will large subsidies announced for oil and gas companies.

We know that there’s a strong narrative about the need to stimulate the economy. But the truth is that using taxpayer dollars to fund a single industry that will accelerate the climate crisis - and whose effects will be worn by small business, travel, and more: that’s not an economy we want.

We don’t think employees should have to choose between pay packet or planet.

We are 3,000 businesses - affected by bushfires, flooding, and COVID - and that’s all only in the last year. We are hurting. But a gas-fired recovery doesn’t help the small businesses in Australia, the tourism sector, or the small makers, baristas and plumbers - it helps make fossil fuel execs richer.

The bottom line: we won’t be an excuse for demanding action on climate.
